# Email Voice & Communication Policies — Gulf Coast Machining Co.

## How We Sound

We're a 26-year-old machine shop, not a tech startup. Emails are short, plain, and friendly. We sound like a person, because a person (usually Cheryl in the office, sometimes Dale) is who customers expect.

Rules:
- Greeting: "Hi [first name]," for regulars, "Hello [name]," for new contacts
- Get to the point in the first sentence
- Short paragraphs, no bullet-point walls in customer emails
- Sign-off: "Thanks," followed by the sender name and "Gulf Coast Machining Co. | (409) 555-0147"
- Never use corporate filler ("per my last email", "circling back", "synergy")
- If we can't do something, say so plainly and offer the closest thing we can do
- Quotes always include: price, lead time, and how long the quote is valid

## Standard Responses

- **Quote requests:** Acknowledge same day even if the full quote takes 24 hrs. "We'll have a number to you by [time]."
- **Lead time questions:** Give the honest range from the pricing sheet. Mention turnaround season if it applies.
- **Complaints:** Apologize once, sincerely. State what we'll do and when. Dale personally follows up on any quality complaint.
- **Rush requests:** Check against rush policy. Be honest if we can't hit the date.
- **Vendor/sales pitches:** Polite decline, no meeting. "We're set on suppliers right now but will keep you on file."

## What We Never Do

- Never share customer names or job details with other customers
- Never quote prices for exotic materials without checking current material cost
- Never commit shop capacity during turnaround season without checking with Dale
