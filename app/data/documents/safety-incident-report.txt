GULF COAST MACHINING CO. - INCIDENT/NEAR MISS REPORT
Form S-3, Rev 2024

Report #: 2026-007
Date of incident: June 2, 2026, approx 2:40 PM
Location: Bay 2, lathe #4 (Mazak QT-350)
Reported by: T. Guidry (shift lead)
Type: NEAR MISS - no injury

DESCRIPTION:
Operator was turning a 14" diameter 4140 steel blank for a flange
order. Chuck jaw retention bolt on jaw #2 backed out during a
roughing pass at 380 RPM. The jaw shifted but did not separate
from the chuck. Operator heard the change in sound, hit e-stop.
Part was scrapped (out of round). No tool or guard contact, no
injury. Machine inspected - chuck removed for service.

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS:
- Retention bolts last torqued at jaw change ~3 weeks prior, no
  log entry found for torque check since
- Torque check is on the monthly PM sheet but Bay 2's May PM
  was signed off with the lathe section left blank
- New operator (4 months) had not been trained to do visual
  jaw bolt check at setup - this is tribal knowledge, not in
  the setup checklist

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS TAKEN:
- Lathe #4 tagged out until chuck serviced and bolts replaced
- All other lathes' chuck bolts torque-verified same day (all OK)

RECOMMENDED CORRECTIVE ACTIONS (open):
1. Add jaw bolt visual check to the written setup checklist
2. Investigate why May PM was signed incomplete
3. Add torque verification to weekly (not monthly) PM for lathes
   running interrupted cuts
4. Brief all operators at Monday toolbox talk
